The 4-Step Path

Pre-approval is the foundation. Everything else builds on it.

Without pre-approval, you can't write competitive offers. With it, you can. Here's how the process actually works.

Pre-qual vs. pre-approval

Know the difference

A "pre-qualification" is a soft estimate based on what you tell the lender. A "pre-approval" is a verified letter based on documents the lender has actually reviewed. Sellers want pre-approval. Pre-qual letters get ignored.

Documents needed

Gather these first

2 years of tax returns + W-2s, 2 most recent pay stubs, 2 months of bank statements, ID, and any debts (student loans, car loans, credit cards). Self-employed? Add 2 years of business tax returns + P&Ls.

Lender match

Match lender to your situation

First-time buyer? CalHFA + FHA expert. Self-employed? Bank-statement loan specialist. ITIN-filer? Credit union with ITIN products. Each lender is different. I introduce you to the right one — for free.

Letter in hand

Get the letter

Standard pre-approval: 3-7 days after documents are submitted. ITIN/non-traditional: 2-3 weeks. Letter is good for 60-90 days. Once you have it, we can write competitive offers immediately.

Pre-approval document checklist

Have these ready before your first lender call. The faster you submit, the faster you get the letter — and the sooner we can shop.

  • Income proof2 most recent pay stubs · 2 years of W-2s · 2 years of tax returns
  • Self-employed (if applicable)2 years of business tax returns · YTD P&L · Business license · Bank statements showing deposits
  • Bank statements2 most recent months for ALL accounts (checking, savings, retirement)
  • IdentificationDriver's license OR passport · SSN OR ITIN card · Permanent resident card (if applicable)
  • Debt detailsStudent loans, car loans, credit cards, child support — anything with a monthly payment
  • Down payment proofWhere did the money come from? Savings (60+ days seasoned), gift letter from family, or 401k loan documentation
  • Rent payment history12 months of canceled checks OR landlord verification letter (helps if your credit is thin)
  • Employment verification letterFrom your employer, on letterhead, confirming role and salary (the lender will request this directly — but having it ready speeds things up)
Why it matters

A pre-approval letter is the price of admission.

In Q1 2026, the median SoCal listing receives 3-8 offers in its first weekend. Sellers don't have time to chase down financing details on every offer — they look at the pre-approval letter and pick the strongest.

Without a letter, your offer goes in a stack labeled "tire-kickers" and is ignored. With one — even at the same offer price as someone else — your offer is taken seriously. This is not optional in SoCal.

The good news: getting a letter costs nothing. It does NOT pull your credit beyond what's needed (one soft pull). It does NOT obligate you to that lender. And it dramatically clarifies your real budget — sometimes higher than you thought, sometimes lower. Either way, you walk in informed.
