# UX Audit — 2026-05-29

Propose-only audit of the top 5 traffic pages on jessetek.net. No HTML/CSS/JS was modified. Findings come from a static code review against `public/{slug}.html`; GA4 / Clarity data were not directly accessible during the audit, so the top-5 list mirrors the canonical funnel: **home, buyer, seller, valuation, rates**.

Confidence key:
- **High** — verified in code (grep / line numbers given). Acting on this is low risk.
- **Medium** — informed hunch from code review. Worth A/B testing before shipping site-wide.
- **Low** — speculation; validate with Clarity/heatmap before changing.

---

## 1. `/` (home) — `public/home.html` (1546 lines)

**Friction points**

1. **Broken `#contact` anchor on the 3 step cards.** Lines 916, 940, 983 wrap each "How It Works" step card in `<a href="#contact">…</a>`, but no element on the page declares `id="contact"`. Clicking a card scrolls to the top of the document instead of the booking CTA. Users who engaged with the most-clicked element get punished.
2. **Long content desert with no subheadings.** The bento section spans lines 263–874 (~600 lines of markup, multiple visual blocks) but only one H2 governs it (line 266). Mobile readers skimming with a screen reader or scroll-by-heading lose orientation.
3. **Single primary CTA above the fold, no anchor fallback.** Hero (line 113) offers only `/zoom` + a small "grab the free guide" link. No way to jump to social proof or the wins ticker for a skeptic who isn't ready for a call yet.

**Fix proposal**
- Add `id="contact"` to the `<section class="cta-section">` opening tag (line 1008) so every `href="#contact"` on the site lands on the booking card. One-line change, fixes home + buyer + seller (25 broken links total across the site).
- Insert one anchor link in the hero secondary slot: `<a href="#wins">See real recent wins ↓</a>` and add `id="wins"` to the wins ticker (line 238). Gives skeptics a path that isn't "book a call."
- Add a hidden-on-desktop H3 (or visible eyebrow + heading) inside the bento subsections to break up the long region.

**Confidence**: High (1 — broken anchor verified by grep), Medium (2, 3).

## 2. `/buyer` — `public/buyer.html` (992 lines)

**Friction points**

1. **11 step cards all link to a broken `#contact` anchor.** Verified: 11 `href="#contact"` references, zero `id="contact"` elements (lines 174, 198, 222, 249, 270, 296, …). Every step card click on the most engagement-heavy section of the page lands at the top of the document. This is the single highest-impact bug found in the audit.
2. **Single hero CTA goes to `#step1`, not a conversion endpoint.** Line 110 — "See the Steps ↓" is a scroll cue, not a conversion CTA. High-intent buyers must scroll past ~400 lines of step content to reach `/zoom`. The pre-approval strip (line 119) is the only conversion CTA in the first viewport on most screens.
3. **The 11 steps are visual divs (`step-card-title`), not real H3s.** Lines 179, 201, 219… render as headings but are plain `<div>`s. Hurts in-page navigation, accessibility scanning, and "jump to heading" Clarity insights.

**Fix proposal**
- Add `id="contact"` to the `<section class="cta-section">` at line 491. Same fix as home — affects 11 broken links here.
- Add a secondary text-link CTA in the hero: `<a href="/zoom">Or book a 15-min call →</a>` directly below the "See the Steps" button (line 113). Gives high-intent users a shortcut without disrupting the scroll-funnel design.
- Promote `.step-card-title` from `<div>` to `<h3>` in the templating step that generates these cards.

**Confidence**: High (1), Medium (2, 3).

## 3. `/seller` — `public/seller.html` (1006 lines)

**Friction points**

1. **Wrong secondary CTA copy.** Line 554: `<a href="/guide" class="cta-btn cta-btn-secondary">Get the Free Buyer Guide</a>` — on a seller page. Sellers don't want a buyer guide, and `/guide` is buyer-focused content. This looks like a copy-paste from `buyer.html` (identical CTA block).
2. **Same broken `#contact` anchor problem** — 11 step cards point to a non-existent anchor (verified via grep).
3. **Page hero CTA is "See the Steps ↓" only** (line 110). Sellers researching valuation tend to be higher-intent than buyers researching the process — yet they get the same passive scroll-cue CTA.

**Fix proposal**
- Change line 554 to point at a seller-relevant lead magnet:
  `<a href="/seller-net" class="cta-btn cta-btn-secondary">Free Net-Proceeds Calculator</a>` or `<a href="/seller-checklist">Get the Free Seller Checklist</a>`.
- Add `id="contact"` to `<section class="cta-section">` at line 499.
- Add an above-the-fold `<a href="/valuation">Get a free 24h CMA →</a>` as a parallel CTA in the hero (line 109). High-intent sellers shouldn't have to scroll 800 lines to find a valuation path.

**Confidence**: High (1 — copy-paste leftover is unambiguous; 2 — broken anchor verified), Medium (3).

## 4. `/valuation` — `public/valuation.html` (1182 lines)

**Friction points**

1. **"Phone or Email" combined into one field** (line 181). `<input type="text" name="contact" placeholder="(562) 609-4200" required autocomplete="tel" inputmode="text">`. The placeholder shows a phone number, the `autocomplete="tel"` hints phone, but the label says "or Email." Users on mobile get the text keyboard (no @ shortcut), and validation downstream has to handle both formats. This is a form-friction smell on the highest-intent page on the site.
2. **Name placeholder is "Jesse Oñate"** (line 177). Using the realtor's own name as the placeholder reads as a bug to first-time visitors — a generic "Jane Doe" or "Your full name" is the convention.
3. **No address validation/autocomplete** on step 1 (line 130). It's the first field of a 3-step funnel and a typo here breaks the comp pull. `autocomplete="street-address"` is set, but there's no Google Places autocomplete or even a city/zip dropdown helper. Bad addresses → bad CMAs → no follow-up.

**Fix proposal**
- Split contact into two fields with conditional logic, OR keep one field but rename the label to "Phone (preferred) or email" and add a tiny radio toggle that swaps `inputmode="tel"` / `inputmode="email"` for mobile keyboard hints.
- Change name placeholder (line 177) to `placeholder="Your full name"` and the contact placeholder to `placeholder="e.g. (562) 555-0123"`.
- Add Google Places address autocomplete to `#vfAddress`. If that's too heavy, at least add a static `<datalist>` of common SoCal cities, or wire client-side validation that ensures the input contains a CA zip.

**Confidence**: High (1, 2 — verified in code). Medium (3 — Places API is a build decision, not a one-line tweak).

## 5. `/rates` — `public/rates.html` (807 lines)

**Friction points**

1. **Lead-capture form is a cross-origin iframe** (line 177, `api.leadconnectorhq.com/widget/form/...`). Clarity, GA4 form events, and any first-party heatmap **cannot see inside this iframe** — a major blindspot on the page whose entire purpose is to convert. The hardcoded `data-height="460"` plus CSS `min-height:200px` will also produce a noticeable CLS jolt as the iframe content loads.
2. **Bottom-of-page "Sign Me Up →" jumps to `#top`** (line 388, anchor verified at line 99). This works, but the scroll lands at the very top of the page — not directly at the form card on the right of the hero. Users who clicked "Sign Me Up" don't want to see the hero text again, they want the form.
3. **TCPA disclosure is collapsed behind `<details>`** (line 195). Federal compliance increasingly expects the SMS consent language to be **visible** at the point of submission, not hidden behind a "Full SMS terms" click. Risk is regulatory, not just UX.

**Fix proposal**
- Replace the LeadConnector iframe with a same-origin form that POSTs to a Vercel function (or LeadConnector's HTTP API). Removes the Clarity blindspot and lets you A/B test fields. If a same-origin form isn't possible short-term, at minimum wire `postMessage` events from the iframe so GA4 sees submit success/failure.
- Add `id="signup"` to `<div class="rates-form-card">` (line 171) and change line 388 to `href="#signup"`. The user lands directly on the form, not on the page top.
- Move the SMS consent line out of the `<details>` summary, render the full TCPA paragraph above the submit button at small font but always visible.

**Confidence**: High (1, 2 — verified). High (3 — TCPA visibility is industry standard).

## Cross-cutting findings

- **Broken `#contact` anchor across home, buyer, seller** (25 links total): single-character fix per page (add `id="contact"` to the existing `<section class="cta-section">`). Cheapest, highest-impact change in the audit. Confidence: **High**.
- **CTA strip on multiple pages shares one stale "6.30%" placeholder** (`<span class="lrc-num" id="lrcNum">6.30%</span>`). If the live-rate fetch fails, every internal CTA shows stale data. Add a `display:none` fallback path or a "—%" placeholder. Confidence: Medium.
- **Live-rate strip and CTA cards are repeated verbatim across pages** (buyer/seller/valuation/home). When the CTA copy needs updating (e.g. the seller-page Buyer-Guide bug), the lack of a partial template means the fix has to be applied N times. Confidence: Medium.

## Notes on this audit's limits

- I could not pull live Microsoft Clarity heatmaps, GA4 event volumes, or Web Vitals percentiles during the audit window. The top-5 page list is an assumption based on the funnel, not measurement.
- All findings above are static-code-review hunches plus a small number of verifiable bugs (broken anchors, copy-paste CTA). Validate any **Medium**-confidence items in Clarity before shipping changes site-wide.
