# UX Audit — 2026-05-15

Source: code/design review of the top 5 estimated pages by traffic. Clarity heatmap (project `wke2elyrvc`) was not directly accessible from this session; page selection follows the `/home`, `/buyer`, `/seller`, `/valuation`, `/rates` shortlist provided in the task brief. Where a finding is supported only by code structure (not session/heatmap data), confidence is marked **medium**. Propose-only — no HTML/CSS/JS was modified.

---

## 1. home.html

- **Two competing hero CTAs.** The hero pairs a primary "Book my free 15-min call" with a tertiary "Or grab the free 5-Mistakes guide" link (~lines 114–117). When the secondary offer sits next to the primary one above the fold, it splits intent and tends to depress the higher-value action (call booking).
  - **Fix:** Demote the guide link to a secondary section further down the page (e.g., adjacent to the value-prop block), leaving only the call CTA in the hero.
  - **Confidence:** medium (code is clear; the conversion impact is the hunch part — would benefit from a Clarity rage-click / dead-click check on the secondary link).

- **No persistent mobile CTA after the hero scrolls off.** Top nav has a phone icon but there's no sticky bottom-of-viewport CTA bar. On long mobile pages, users who scroll past the hero have to scroll all the way back up to act.
  - **Fix:** Add a sticky bottom CTA band on mobile only (hidden on desktop) that surfaces after the hero leaves the viewport — single "Book Call" button with phone fallback.
  - **Confidence:** medium.

---

## 2. buyer.html

- **Hero CTA is an anchor, not a conversion action.** The hero "See the Steps" button jumps to `#step1` (line ~110). The actual booking CTA ("Book a Strategy Call") doesn't appear until line ~542 — ~80% of the way down a 992-line page. Visitors who land from an ad and don't scroll get no conversion opportunity.
  - **Fix:** Add a sticky mobile CTA bar that appears after the hero leaves the viewport, OR add a second "Book a Call" button inline in the hero alongside "See the Steps."
  - **Confidence:** high (visible in code).

- **Multi-step form step 2 collects beds/baths/timeline with no justification microcopy.** Step 2 has 3 select fields and no "why we're asking" text — that's the step most likely to bleed dropoff because the user already gave their address on step 1 and hasn't yet been asked for contact info, so they have no investment to protect.
  - **Fix:** Add a single line above step 2 ("Helps me pull the right comps before we talk — takes 10 seconds"), OR move the contact step before the qualifier step so commitment increases monotonically.
  - **Confidence:** medium (form structure is visible; dropoff rate is the hunch — verify in Clarity step-by-step funnel).

---

## 3. seller.html

- **Secondary CTA on the seller page sends visitors to the BUYER guide.** Line 555 reads `Get the Free Buyer Guide` linking to `/guide`, which `public/guide.html` confirms is the "5 Mistakes First-Time Homebuyers Make" PDF — content explicitly for buyers. A seller who clicks this is sent off-funnel.
  - **Fix:** Either (a) remove the secondary CTA on seller.html so the only action is "Book a Strategy Call," or (b) ship a seller-specific lead magnet (e.g., "Seller's Pricing & Prep Checklist") and point this button at it.
  - **Confidence:** high (clear content/audience mismatch in the code).

- **Same anchor-CTA-in-hero pattern as buyer.html.** Hero links to `#step1`; the real booking CTA is at line ~542 of a 1006-line page.
  - **Fix:** Same as buyer.html — sticky mobile CTA bar after hero, or inline "Book a Call" button in the hero next to the steps anchor.
  - **Confidence:** high.

---

## 4. valuation.html

- **No fallback escape on the multi-step valuation form.** The form has a progress bar and inline validation, but no "talk to Jesse instead" affordance between steps. The address step in particular is high-friction (sellers are protective about address before they trust the recipient), and users who hesitate have no lower-commitment path.
  - **Fix:** Add a small text link below the step 2 / step 3 form ("Prefer a human estimate? Text Jesse instead → [tel link]") so hesitant users have a secondary funnel.
  - **Confidence:** medium (form structure is visible; the hesitation is the hunch — Clarity scroll/abandon data on the valuation form would confirm).

- **Exit-intent recovery fires desktop-only.** Code review shows exit-intent modal logic gated to desktop (mouse-leave at viewport top). Mobile users — likely the larger share of traffic — get no recovery offer when they bounce.
  - **Fix:** Trigger the same offer on mobile using a different signal (history.back, 30s of inactivity, or scroll-up after scroll-down on the form section).
  - **Confidence:** medium.

---

## 5. rates.html

- **Third-party form iframe is the primary conversion path with no synchronous fallback.** The LeadConnector iframe (~line 177) is positioned as the hero conversion action. If the iframe is slow to load (or blocked), the user sees the trust copy but no usable form, with no inline fallback (phone, email, or native form).
  - **Fix:** Add a `loading="lazy"` or `loading="eager"` attribute as appropriate, and add a visible fallback CTA above the iframe — "Trouble loading? Text me at [tel link]" — so the page still converts when the embed fails.
  - **Confidence:** medium (load failure mode is the hunch — verify in Clarity sessions where users sit on rates.html with no interaction, or check Web Vitals LCP for iframe-related delay).

- **Bottom "Sign Me Up →" anchor scrolls users back to a form that's already above the fold.** Line ~388 is a CTA that scrolls to `#top` where the iframe already lives. This is a no-op for desktop viewers who can see the form, and on mobile it pulls them away from the FAQ they were just reading.
  - **Fix:** Replace bottom "Sign Me Up" with a distinct secondary funnel — e.g., "Still have questions? → Book a 15-min call (/zoom)" — so the page has two real options instead of one repeated one.
  - **Confidence:** high.

---

## Cross-page patterns

1. **Primary CTAs are far below the fold on long pages** (buyer, seller) — sticky mobile CTA bar is the lowest-effort fix and applies to both.
2. **Multi-step forms lack escape hatches** (valuation, buyer) — adding a "text Jesse instead" path costs little and recovers hesitant users.
3. **Audience/content mismatches** (seller.html → buyer guide) suggest a quick label audit across all CTA buttons would catch others.

## Data caveats

This audit is code-only. Heatmap data from Clarity (`wke2elyrvc`) was not pulled in this session, and GA4 traffic-ranking data was not queried — so the top-5 list and the "below-fold dropoff" / "mobile bounce" claims are structural inferences, not measured ones. Before shipping any of the medium-confidence fixes, verify in Clarity:
- Scroll depth on home/buyer/seller (does the booking CTA actually get seen?)
- Step-by-step funnel on valuation and buyer-quiz forms
- Rage-click / dead-click density on the rates.html iframe area
