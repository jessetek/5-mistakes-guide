# UX Audit — 2026-05-08

Source: code/design review of public/{slug}.html files. No live Clarity or GA4 data was accessed (Clarity at https://clarity.microsoft.com/projects/view/wke2elyrvc requires auth). Confidence below reflects whether each finding was verified in code (high) or is a design-review hunch (medium/low).

Pages audited (top-5 guess per spec): `home`, `buyer`, `seller`, `valuation`, `rates`.

---

## 1. /home (public/home.html — 1545 lines)

**Friction**

- **Hero has no on-page conversion option.** Primary CTA `Book my free 15-min call` (line 114) routes to `/zoom`, a separate page. Secondary link points to `/guide` — also off-page. Visitors who aren't ready to commit to a Zoom have no inline form/quiz/calculator to engage with above the fold. *(medium — hunch; would want scroll-depth + bounce data from GA4 to confirm)*
- **3 step cards link to `#contact` but no `id="contact"` exists on the page.** Verified via grep — anchor target is missing, so clicking those cards scrolls to top instead of to a contact form. *(high — verified)*

**Fix proposals**

- Add a lightweight inline opt-in (e.g., the rates SMS form or the buyer-quiz first question) into the hero or directly below the trust strip, so users have a 2-second commitment option before being asked for a 15-min call.
- Either add `id="contact"` to the page's footer-CTA section, or rewrite the 3 `href="#contact"` links to point at `/zoom` or `/valuation` so the click does something.

## 2. /buyer (public/buyer.html — 992 lines)

**Friction**

- **11 of 11 step cards link to `#contact` — anchor does not exist.** Every "Step XX" tile is wrapped in `<a href="#contact">` (lines 174, 198, 222, 249, 270, 296, 314, 342, 363, 389, 412). Grep confirms no `id="contact"` anywhere in the file. The entire roadmap is effectively a list of dead clicks that scroll the user back to top. *(high — verified)*
- **Above-fold CTA is `See the Steps ↓` (line 110), not a conversion action.** The first lead-capture CTA (`Book a Strategy Call`) sits ~440 lines down at line 542. A user who lands cold has no commitment ladder above the fold — only a "scroll more" button. *(medium — hunch; Clarity rage-click data would confirm)*

**Fix proposals**

- Add `id="contact"` to the existing `Book a Strategy Call` section near line 542, OR change all 11 `href="#contact"` to `/zoom` (or `#step1` if the intent was just to jump into the roadmap).
- Add a secondary inline CTA in the hero (e.g., "Take the 60-second buyer-readiness quiz →" linking to `/buyer-quiz`) so above-fold users have a low-friction conversion option without scrolling.

## 3. /seller (public/seller.html — 1006 lines)

**Friction**

- **Same broken anchor as /buyer: 11 step cards → `#contact`, no `id="contact"` on page.** Mirror structure of buyer.html. *(high — verified via grep)*
- **No above-fold lead-gen for sellers despite this being the highest-intent persona.** Sellers landing here see narrative content, not the `/valuation` CMA form which is the actual conversion. The "Free Valuation" CTA isn't surfaced in the hero. *(medium — hunch)*

**Fix proposals**

- Add `id="contact"` to the page's `Book a Strategy Call` section, OR rewrite the 11 anchors to `/valuation` so a step-card click drops the seller directly into the CMA form (their highest-value action).
- Add a secondary hero link: `Or get your home value in 60 sec → /valuation`.

## 4. /valuation (public/valuation.html — 1290 lines) — primary lead form

**Friction**

- **Step 3 "Phone or Email" input is misconfigured.** Line 199: `<input type="text" name="contact" placeholder="(562) 609-4200" required autocomplete="tel" inputmode="text">`. The placeholder is a phone number, `autocomplete="tel"` says "phone," but `inputmode="text"` forces mobile users to a generic keyboard. Worse, accepting phone OR email in one field is ambiguous (no visible hint that either is valid) and complicates server-side validation/routing in `/api/valuation`. *(high — verified)*
- **Live rate strip (lines 305+) appears immediately below the hero/form.** It competes for attention with the form's submit moment and may pull users away to `/rates` before they finish the CMA. *(low/medium — hunch)*

**Fix proposals**

- Split the contact field into two optional inputs: `Phone (we'll text you)` with `type="tel" inputmode="tel" autocomplete="tel"` AND `Email` with `type="email" inputmode="email" autocomplete="email"`, requiring at least one. OR keep one field but switch to `type="tel"`/`inputmode="tel"` and update the label to `Phone (preferred) or Email` with two placeholder examples.
- Move the live-rate strip below the form's success section, or replace it with a "what happens next" panel that reinforces the just-submitted action instead of pulling the user to a different page.

## 5. /rates (public/rates.html — 811 lines)

**Friction**

- **Form is a third-party iframe (LeadConnector, line 181-196).** Cross-origin iframes are invisible to Clarity heatmaps and most analytics — you can see clicks *to* the iframe but not field-level drop-off, error states, or partial completions. The single highest-leverage CRO surface on this page is currently a black box. *(medium — verified)*
- **TCPA SMS disclosure is collapsed inside a `<details>` element (lines 199-204) that defaults closed.** Federal TCPA "clear and conspicuous" guidance for SMS marketing consent generally expects the disclosure to be visible at the point of opt-in, not hidden behind a click. Also a soft-trust hit at the moment of submit. *(medium — hunch on legal risk; design hunch on trust)*

**Fix proposals**

- Replace the iframe with a native form posting to a webhook endpoint (or use LeadConnector's POST API) so Clarity/GA4 can instrument field interaction. If the iframe must stay, add cross-iframe postMessage events for "field focused" / "form submitted" so funnel drop-off is observable.
- Either expand the TCPA disclosure inline by default (small grey text under the submit button), or move the key consent line ("By submitting, you agree to receive ~4 SMS/mo, reply STOP to opt out") above the submit button and keep only the longform terms in `<details>`.

## Cross-page observation (low confidence, not counted in per-page findings)

`.btn-primary` across all 5 pages does not appear to define a `:focus-visible` style in inline CSS. Worth verifying in `css/main.min.css` — if missing, keyboard users get no focus indicator on the page's primary action. *(low — would need to read the bundled CSS to confirm)*

| /buyer | broken `#contact` anchors (11) | no above-fold commitment option |
